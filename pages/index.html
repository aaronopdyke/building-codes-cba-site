Do building codes pay off?

A cost-benefit explorer for adopting and enforcing stronger building codes — avoided damage, lives, jobs and growth.

How the tool works

1

Model today's buildings

Floor areas and building counts on a 1 km grid, valued with unit construction costs and the local mix of building types. The existing stock's code levels come from construction vintages blended with performance-based judgments of design practice (none → low → moderate → high code).

2

Stress-test them

Hazard maps meet vulnerability functions per building class (currently earthquake) — giving average annual damage and expected fatalities for the stock as it is, and as it would be under a stronger code, with compliance scaled by a building practice factor.

3

Grow the stock

New construction follows all five SSP growth scenarios to 2100; the existing stock turns over gradually. Only new (and rebuilt) floor area pays the code premium — priced on the structural share of construction cost — and only it earns the stronger code's protection.

4

Count all three dividends

One benefit-cost ratio counts the Triple Dividend of Resilience: avoided damage (plus avoided reconstruction carbon), unlocked economic potential (investment stimulus, jobs), and co-benefits (durability). Lives and healthy life-years are counted, never monetised. Choose a 25-, 50- or 75-year horizon.

Sources: GEM hazard, exposure, vulnerability and carbon accounting (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0) · GHSL built-up vintages · World Bank boundaries and indicators · GFDRR Building Regulations Atlas. Full method & caveats on the About page.
